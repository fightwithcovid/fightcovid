Fact Checks, Busting Myths!

FAKE NEWS:

We all are good at sharing stuff and mostly the false stuff. These fake messages are made viral on WhatsApp groups which could be dangerous ahead. Stay away from rumours and myths...

Myth Busters:

• Cold weather CANNOT kill the Coronavirus.

• Corona virus cannot be killed by use of hairdryers.

• Garlic cannot prevent Covid-19.

• Thermal scanners can only detect fever. They don't detect whether or not someone has the virus.

• Coronavirus cannot be transmitted through mosquito bites.

• No medicine or vaccine is available till date.

• Drinking alcohol does not protect one from the virus.

• Antibiotics are ineffective against the virus.

So how are we suppose to protect ourselves?

Here is a list of preacutions that can be taken:


Some Precautions established by WHO and Government of India:

1) Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, especially after you have been in a public place or after blowing your nose, coughing or sneezing.

2) Maintain at least 1 meter (3 feet) distance between yourself and anyone who is coughing or sneezing.

3) Cover your mouth and nose with a tissue when you cough or sneeze or use the inside of your elbow.

4) If you have fever, cough and difficulty in breathing, seek medical care early.

5) Clean and disinfect touched surfaces frequently.

6) Avoid touching your eyes, nose and mouth.

7) Stay home if you feel unwell.

8) Wear a facemask if you are sick.

9) Avoid Mass Gathering.

Know the zones:

Red Zones:

These are the districts indentified taing into account the total number of active cases, doubling rate of confirmed cases, extent of testing and surveillance feedback from the districts.

Activities Prohibited:

1) Running of taxis and and auto rickshaws

2) Intra-district and inter-district plying of buses.

3) Shops in urban areas, for non-essential goods,malls, markets and market complexes.

Activities Allowed:

1) A maximum of 2 persons (besides the driver) in four-wheeler vehicles, and with no pillion rider in the case of two-wheelers allowed only for permitted activities.

2) Manufacturing units of essential goods.

3) All standalone, neighborhood and shops in residential complexes to remain open in urban areas, without any distinction of essential and non-essential.

4) E-Commerce activities, in the Red Zones, are permitted only in respect of essential goods.

Know the zones:

Green Zones:

Districts with zero confirmed cases till date or no confirmed cases in the last 21 days.

Activites Allowed:

In the Green Zones, all activities are permitted except the limited number of activities which are prohibited throughout the country, irrespective of the Zone.

1)Buses can operate with upto 50% seating capacity and bus depots can operate with upto 50% capacity.

2)Social Distancing and other precautionary norms to be followed.

Know the zones:

Orange Zones:

Those districts, which are neither defined as Red nor Green, shall be classified as Orange zones.

Activities Prohibited:

1)Inter ans Intra district plying of buses.

Activities allowed:

In addition to activities permitted in Red Zone. 1)Taxis and cab aggregators will be permitted with 1 driver and 2 passengers only.

2)Inter-district movement of individuals and vehicles will be allowed for permitted activities only.
